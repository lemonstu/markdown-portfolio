Every Tuesday

What the weekly brief includes.

Six analyst lenses, one ranked action list, follow-up tracking on the previous week's recommendations. White-label PDF, editable Google Doc, and a forwardable email summary.

  • Executive summary

    The three-sentence version of the week. Clicks, impressions, what moved, and the top priorities.

  • Top 3 urgent risks

    Page-specific drops with magnitude, likely cause flagged (position, CTR, indexation, cannibalization, seasonal), and recommended action.

  • Top 5 growth opportunities

    Highest-leverage wins available this week — CTR rewrites, near-win expansions, decay refreshes — with finding, evidence, and recommended action for each.

  • Near-win keywords (positions 4–20)

    The highest-ROI zone in SEO. Top 10 queries by volume, current position, target position, and lift action.

  • Content decay & cannibalization

    Declining pages flagged against a 90-day baseline; multiple-URL conflicts on the same query with a clear consolidate/differentiate call.

  • Internal-link recommendations

    Specific source → target pairs with anchor suggestions, sourced from a one-time site map built during onboarding.

  • GA4 engagement / conversion mismatch

    Pages where engagement and conversion are out of step — measurement problem, placement problem, or intent mismatch — with the diagnosis attached.

  • Action list with priority, impact, effort

    5–10 actions per brief, each scored P1/P2/P3 with Impact and Effort on a 1–5 scale and an expected timeframe to observe results.

FAQ

Common questions.

How is this different from Looker Studio, Semrush, or Ahrefs?

Those are tools — they show metrics or surface keyword data. The brief is a delivered analyst artifact built specifically for one client site, with a ranked action list and follow-up tracking on previous weeks' recommendations. Most agencies we work with run all of the above; the brief is the missing layer between the data and the work.

Is the brief AI-generated?

No. Detection runs on deterministic rules against GSC and GA4 data. AI assists with prose drafting only. Every recommendation is reviewed by a human analyst before delivery, and every finding traces back to a specific row in the source data.

What access do you need from our clients?

Read-only Google Search Console access (Restricted user) and Viewer-level GA4 access. No CMS, server, or admin credentials. Both are revocable in one click. We provide a step-by-step access guide for your team and your clients.

Can we fully white-label the brief?

Yes. The PDF, Google Doc, and email summary all carry your agency's logo, colors, and "Prepared by" line. We do not appear anywhere in the client-facing deliverable. We can also operate inside a Slack channel that's invisible to your clients.

What kind of sites is this not a fit for?

Sites with fewer than ~1,500 monthly clicks in GSC, sites under six months old, sites without GA4 configured, and sites where most traffic is paid rather than organic. We flag these in qualification and refund if we miss it.

Do you implement the recommendations?

No. The brief is the strategic layer — what to fix, update, or publish next. Execution stays with your team or your client's team, which keeps the margin where you want it.

What happens after the four weeks?

At the wrap-up call we offer monthly recurring options if you want to continue — typically single-site, 3-site, or 5-site packs. There's no auto-renewal and no obligation. If the pilot didn't earn its keep, we shake hands and part ways.

What if we don't see traffic growth in 30 days?

The brief is sold on clarity and decisions, not on guaranteed traffic outcomes — SEO is too slow and too variable to honestly promise a number in four weeks. What the pilot delivers is a documented baseline, four weeks of prioritized action lists, and a 90-day roadmap. If that's not more useful than your current reporting workflow by week two, we refund.
