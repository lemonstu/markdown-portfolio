## 1. Brutal market judgment

**Is this a real business opportunity?**
Yes — but narrowly. The opportunity is a *retainer-priced human service for agencies*, not a SaaS dashboard, not a tool for SMBs. Agencies have the budget, the recurring need, and the white-label motive. Everyone else is a distraction.

**Weakest version (avoid):**
- A dashboard with "AI insights" sold directly to small business owners. They won't act on the report, won't pay >$99/mo, and will churn in 60 days.
- A free AI tool with paid upgrade. The market is saturated and your CAC will be brutal.
- A self-serve SaaS launched before you've manually delivered the work 30+ times. You will build the wrong product.

**Strongest version:**
A done-for-you white-label weekly action brief for SEO/content agencies, priced $400–$800/mo per client site, sold as a 4-week paid pilot first. Agency rebrands it and resells it. You're invisible. You're the analyst they wish they had on staff.

**Who is most likely to pay:**
1. SEO agency owners managing 8–40 client sites, billing $1.5k–$5k/mo retainers, who are drowning in monthly report production.
2. Content agencies / WordPress agencies who *should* be doing SEO but don't have an in-house analyst.
3. Solo SEO consultants managing 5–15 retainer clients who use reporting as their differentiator.
4. Niche publishers with $20k+/mo revenue who treat SEO as a P&L line.

**Why agencies pay:**
- It removes 4–8 hours of manual reporting per client per month.
- It gives them something to *show clients weekly* (a retention lever, not a tool).
- It makes their junior staff look senior.
- It justifies a price increase to their own clients.
- The math is obvious: pay you $500, charge their client $1,500.

**Why small businesses are weak buyers:**
- They don't have GA4/GSC properly configured.
- They don't have the staff to execute recommendations.
- They confuse "report" with "results" and churn when traffic doesn't move in 30 days.
- $300/mo feels expensive to them; $300/mo per client is rounding error to an agency.

**Biggest failure risks:**
1. **You undercharge.** $99/mo turns this into a treadmill. Anchor at $400+ from day one.
2. **You sell to the wrong buyer.** Small business owners will eat your time and churn.
3. **You drown in delivery.** No SOP, no templates, no QA → 6 hours per report → unprofitable.
4. **You build software too early.** You will build the wrong features because you've never delivered the service at scale.
5. **You generate generic "increase content quality" recommendations.** Agencies will spot this in week 2 and cancel.
6. **You over-promise traffic gains.** SEO is slow. Sell the *process and clarity*, not the outcome.

**What must be proven before building software:**
- 5+ paid pilots completed end-to-end.
- 3+ pilots converted to monthly recurring.
- Average delivery time per report under 90 minutes.
- Repeatable analysis logic documented and battle-tested.
- A clear answer to "what would I automate first?" backed by real time-tracking data.

**Verdict:** Real business. Modest ceiling as a service (~$30k–$80k MRR before it becomes an agency-management problem), much higher ceiling if you eventually productize the *analyst* not the dashboard. Sell the service this month.

## 6. Weekly report template

> Each brief follows the same structure. The structure is the product.

### Section 1 — Cover / Header
- **Purpose:** Set context in 5 seconds.
- **Data used:** Site URL, reporting period, agency logo, comparison baselines.
- **Shows:** Site name, "Week of [date range]," compared to [prior 7d] and [prior 28d avg].
- **Example wording:** *"Organic Growth Brief — example.com — Week of May 6–12, 2026"*
- **Avoid misleading:** Always show the comparison window explicitly. Never hide the baseline.

### Section 2 — Executive summary (≤4 sentences)
- **Purpose:** Tell the client the week's story before they read anything else.
- **Data used:** Aggregate clicks, impressions, CTR, avg position, GA4 sessions, conversions, all vs. baseline.
- **Shows:** Direction of change + the 1–2 things that mattered.
- **Example:** *"Clicks down 6% week-over-week, driven mostly by a 28% drop on `/guides/X` after losing position 3 on its primary query. Two new keywords entered top 10 on `/blog/Y`. Recommended action: prioritize the title rewrite on `/guides/X` and monitor `/blog/Y` for sustained ranking."*
- **Avoid misleading:** Don't celebrate noise. A 4% WoW swing on low-volume sites is not news. State magnitudes and baselines.

### Section 3 — Top 3 urgent risks
- **Purpose:** What needs attention this week.
- **Data used:** GSC click/impression drops, ranking drops on top-20 pages, GA4 traffic drops.
- **Shows:** Page URL, what dropped, by how much, suspected cause, recommended action.
- **Example:** *"`/templates/A` — clicks down 38% WoW (from 412 to 256). Impressions stable. CTR dropped from 4.1% to 2.6%. Likely cause: title tag change detected on May 9. Recommendation: revert title or test new variation."*
- **Avoid misleading:** Always distinguish "ranking lost" from "search interest dropped" from "CTR dropped." Different problems, different fixes.

### Section 4 — Top 5 growth opportunities
- **Purpose:** Where the highest-leverage wins are this week.
- **Data used:** Position 4–20 queries with rising impressions, pages with high impressions and low CTR, content decay candidates.
- **Shows:** Opportunity type, page/query, current state, recommended action, expected impact range.
- **Example:** *"`/learn/Z` — ranks position 6 for 'how to do Z' (2,100 impressions/wk, CTR 1.9% — expected ~5% at this position). Title is generic. Recommended rewrite included. Expected lift: +60–80 clicks/wk."*
- **Avoid misleading:** Be honest about expected ranges. Use observed CTR curves for the site, not industry averages.

### Section 5 — Pages losing clicks
- **Purpose:** Identify retention work.
- **Data used:** GSC page-level click delta vs. prior 7d and prior 28d avg.
- **Shows:** Table of top 10 pages by absolute click loss, with % change and likely cause flag.
- **Example wording for flagging:** *"Probable cause: position drop / CTR drop / indexation / cannibalization / seasonal."*
- **Avoid misleading:** Don't list pages that lost clicks due to seasonality without saying so. Mark seasonal flags clearly.

### Section 6 — Pages with rising impressions but weak CTR
- **Purpose:** Highest-ROI quick wins.
- **Data used:** GSC page+query data; impressions up >20% WoW, CTR more than 30% below expected at current avg position.
- **Shows:** Table of URL, query, impressions, current CTR, expected CTR, recommended title/meta change.
- **Example:** *"`/compare/X-vs-Y` — query 'X vs Y comparison,' 1,820 impressions (+34% WoW), CTR 1.4% (expected ~3.8% at avg pos 7.2). Title rewrite recommended."*
- **Avoid misleading:** Don't recommend CTR rewrites on branded queries. Filter those out before the table.

### Section 7 — Keywords in positions 4–20
- **Purpose:** The highest-leverage SEO action zone.
- **Data used:** GSC query data, position 4–20, minimum 100 impressions in the period.
- **Shows:** Top 15 queries by impression volume, with current position, target position, and lift action (content, internal links, CTR).
- **Avoid misleading:** Distinguish queries the page already targets vs. accidental ranks. Different recommendations.

### Section 8 — Content decay opportunities
- **Purpose:** Find pages that were performing and aren't anymore.
- **Data used:** GSC 28-day vs. trailing 90-day clicks/impressions on pages with >500 historical clicks.
- **Shows:** Top 5 decaying pages with severity, suspected cause, refresh vs. expand vs. consolidate vs. retire recommendation.
- **Example:** *"`/posts/2023-trends` — clicks down 64% vs. 90-day baseline. Cause: content dated, competitor `[domain]` updated. Recommendation: refresh date, add 2026 section, update internal links."*
- **Avoid misleading:** Don't flag intentionally evergreen-but-low-volume content as decay.

### Section 9 — Cannibalization risks
- **Purpose:** Find multiple URLs competing for one query.
- **Data used:** GSC query-page mapping where >1 URL appears for the same query with significant impressions.
- **Shows:** Query, conflicting URLs, dominant URL, recommendation (consolidate / differentiate intent / canonical).
- **Avoid misleading:** Don't flag legitimate site-section conflicts (e.g., category page + product page on a clearly different intent).

### Section 10 — Internal link recommendations
- **Purpose:** Quick on-site wins.
- **Data used:** GSC + a one-time content map (built in onboarding). Pages targeting positions 4–20 cross-referenced against pages that mention the query and don't link to it yet.
- **Shows:** Source page → target page, anchor text suggestion, why.
- **Avoid misleading:** Don't recommend links between unrelated pages. Verify topical relevance.

### Section 11 — GA4 engagement / conversion mismatches
- **Purpose:** Catch pages that get traffic but don't convert (or convert but get no traffic).
- **Data used:** GA4 sessions, engagement rate, key event count by landing page (last 14 days).
- **Shows:** 5 mismatch pages with type (high engagement / no conversion, low engagement / high conversion, etc.) and likely cause.
- **Avoid misleading:** Verify conversion event setup. If suspicious, flag *measurement problem* before assuming page problem.

### Section 12 — Previous actions follow-up
- **Purpose:** Show the agency that recommendations get tracked.
- **Data used:** Action log from prior briefs, current week's metrics for the affected pages.
- **Shows:** Status (done / in progress / not started) and observed impact (positive / negative / no change yet / too soon).
- **Avoid misleading:** SEO changes take time. State "expected timeframe for observation" honestly.

### Section 13 — This week's recommended action list
- **Purpose:** The whole point of the brief.
- **Data used:** Synthesized from all prior sections.

- **Avoid misleading:** Don't promise findings. Just list what's being watched.

## 8. Analysis and scoring framework

Use deterministic rules. AI explains. AI never invents.

**Common conventions:**
- *Recent window:* last 7 days
- *Prior window:* days 8–14
- *Long baseline:* trailing 28-day average (excluding most recent 7)
- *Severity:* 1 (note) → 5 (urgent)
- *Impact:* 1 (negligible) → 5 (significant traffic / conversion shift expected)
- *Effort:* 1 (under 30 min) → 5 (multi-day project)
- Minimum data threshold: page must have ≥30 clicks or ≥500 impressions in either window to enter detection (otherwise filed under "low signal").

---

### Detector 1 — Traffic drop
- **Input:** GSC page-level clicks (recent vs. prior + 28-day baseline)
- **Comparison:** Recent vs. prior 7d; recent vs. 28d trailing avg
- **Rule:** Flag if recent clicks ≤ 70% of prior AND ≤ 75% of 28d baseline AND absolute delta ≥ 30 clicks
- **Severity:** 5 if ≥50% drop on a top-20 traffic page; 3 if 30–49% on top-50; 2 otherwise
- **Impact:** 4–5 if page generated >2% of site clicks last 28d; 2–3 otherwise
- **Effort:** depends on root cause (see "suggested action")
- **Suggested action:** "Investigate cause: position drop / CTR drop / indexation / cannibalization / seasonal."
- **Example wording:** *"`/guides/X` lost 41% of clicks WoW (612 → 361). Position dropped from 3.2 to 6.8 on the primary query. Likely cause: competitor content update. Recommended action: refresh content this week."*
- **False positive risks:** seasonality, Google Discover variability, branded query loss after PR shifts, holiday weeks
- **Manual QA:** check seasonal trend on the page over last 12 months; check if site-wide clicks dropped proportionally (sitewide event, not page-specific)

### Detector 2 — CTR opportunity
- **Input:** GSC page+query data
- **Comparison:** Observed CTR vs. expected CTR at the current avg position (use site-specific CTR curve when ≥3 months data available; otherwise use a conservative default curve)
- **Rule:** Flag query+page combos where impressions ≥ 500 (last 7d) AND observed CTR ≤ 50% of expected CTR AND avg position between 3 and 15
- **Severity:** by impression volume bucket (≥2000: 5, 1000–1999: 4, 500–999: 3)
- **Impact:** estimated lift = (expected CTR – observed CTR) × impressions
- **Effort:** 1 (title/meta rewrite)
- **Suggested action:** title rewrite, meta rewrite, schema add, FAQ block
- **Example wording:** *"Query 'X' on `/page`: avg position 6.0, CTR 1.8% (expected ~5.2%). Title rewrite recommended. Expected lift: +60–90 clicks/wk."*
- **False positive risks:** branded queries (always low CTR off-brand pages); SERP feature presence (zero-click queries); image/video carousels
- **Manual QA:** exclude branded queries; check SERP for feature snippets visually

### Detector 3 — Keyword near-win (position 4–20)
- **Input:** GSC query data
- **Comparison:** current position vs. prior 28d average
- **Rule:** Flag queries where (a) position between 4–20 AND (b) impressions ≥ 100/wk AND (c) page already targets the query (verify by checking on-page presence of head term).
- **Severity:** 3–4 based on impression volume
- **Impact:** 3–5 (top of leverage curve)
- **Effort:** 2–4 (content addition or internal links)
- **Suggested action:** content expansion, internal-link sprint, FAQ block, related query coverage
- **Example wording:** *"`/guide/Y` ranks position 7 for 'how to do Y' (1,200 imp/wk). Add 2 sub-sections covering 'beginner' and 'cost' angles; internal-link from `/blog/Y-intro`."*
- **False positive risks:** seasonal queries; query the page doesn't actually target
- **Manual QA:** Ctrl-F the query phrase on the page; verify intent match

### Detector 4 — Content decay
- **Input:** GSC clicks (last 28d) vs. trailing 90d/180d baseline
- **Rule:** Page historically had ≥500 cumulative clicks AND last 28d ≤ 70% of 90d trailing average AND not flagged seasonal
- **Severity:** 3–5 based on magnitude of decline
- **Impact:** 4
- **Effort:** 3–5 (refresh, expand, or consolidate)
- **Suggested action:** refresh / expand / consolidate / retire
- **Example wording:** *"`/post/Z` clicks down 52% vs 90d baseline. Competitor update detected. Refresh recommended."*
- **False positive risks:** seasonal topics, dated content meant to be retired, sites with traffic shifts caused by Discover not Search
- **Manual QA:** check whether page is intentionally seasonal/dated; check 12-month trend

### Detector 5 — Cannibalization
- **Input:** GSC query-page data
- **Rule:** Same query has ≥2 URLs with ≥50 impressions each in last 28d, both ranking within top 30, AND content overlap detected (manual check or simple TF-IDF if automated)
- **Severity:** 3–4
- **Impact:** 3
- **Effort:** 3 (consolidate, differentiate, canonical, redirect)
- **Suggested action:** consolidate / differentiate by intent / 301 / canonical
- **Example wording:** *"Query 'A' has `/url-1` (pos 5) and `/url-2` (pos 14) both ranking. Recommendation: consolidate into `/url-1`, 301 `/url-2`."*
- **False positive risks:** legitimate intent differences (category vs. product vs. comparison)
- **Manual QA:** read both pages; confirm intent overlap

### Detector 6 — Internal link gap
- **Input:** Page X targets query Y (positions 4–20). Search the site for pages that mention Y but don't link to X.
- **Rule:** Mentions exist AND no current internal link AND mentioning page has topical relevance
- **Severity:** 2–3
- **Impact:** 2–3
- **Effort:** 1
- **Suggested action:** add internal link from mentioning page with descriptive anchor
- **Example wording:** *"`/blog/A` mentions 'X topic' but doesn't link to `/guides/X`. Add anchor 'how to X'."*
- **False positive risks:** unrelated mentions, navigation-only mentions
- **Manual QA:** open both pages; verify relevance

### Detector 7 — High-traffic low-conversion page
- **Input:** GA4 sessions + conversions by landing page
- **Rule:** Page sessions ≥ 200/14d AND conversion rate ≤ 25% of site-average conversion rate for that page type
- **Severity:** 3
- **Impact:** 3–4
- **Effort:** 2–4
- **Suggested action:** CRO test, conversion path audit, CTA reposition, intent mismatch investigation
- **Example wording:** *"`/page` has 1,200 sessions in 14d but only 4 conversions vs. site average of 24."*
- **False positive risks:** measurement problems (event misfire), intent mismatch
- **Manual QA:** verify event firing in GA4 DebugView before recommending CRO

### Detector 8 — Page that needs refresh
- **Input:** Content decay (Detector 4) + age signals (last modified date, dated language)
- **Rule:** Decay flagged AND content older than 12 months AND topic is time-sensitive
- **Severity:** 3
- **Impact:** 4
- **Effort:** 3
- **Suggested action:** content refresh with updated dates, new product mentions, restructured intro
- **Example wording:** *"`/post/Z` last updated Sept 2024. Refresh with current pricing, new products, updated year-in-title."*
- **False positive risks:** intentionally evergreen content; content the agency has scheduled for retirement
- **Manual QA:** confirm topic is time-sensitive; check competitor update dates

### Detector 9 — Page that deserves expansion
- **Input:** GSC query data — multiple related queries ranking in positions 10–25 on a single page
- **Rule:** ≥3 distinct related queries (semantically related, not just morphological variants) with ≥100 imp each ranking 10–25 on the same page
- **Severity:** 2–3
- **Impact:** 4
- **Effort:** 3–4
- **Suggested action:** add new sections targeting each related query family
- **Example wording:** *"`/guide/Y` is ranking for 3 related query clusters (beginner, cost, comparison) in positions 11–18. Expand page with 3 new sections."*
- **False positive risks:** page is already long and would dilute primary intent
- **Manual QA:** read the page; assess whether expansion harms primary intent

### Detector 10 — New content opportunity
- **Input:** GSC queries the site ranks for in positions 20–50 with growing impressions AND no dedicated page targeting them
- **Rule:** Query impressions trending up (last 4 weeks); no page on the site primarily targets the query
- **Severity:** 2
- **Impact:** 4–5 (long-term)
- **Effort:** 4 (new content)
- **Suggested action:** brief and produce dedicated content
- **Example wording:** *"Query 'X' has rising impressions (320 → 640 → 980 over 3 weeks). Site has no dedicated page. Recommend new content."*
- **False positive risks:** seasonal spikes, query already covered by an existing page that just needs strengthening
- **Manual QA:** site-search the query; verify no near-coverage exists

### Detector 11 — Previous action follow-up
- **Input:** Action log from prior briefs + current week metrics on affected URLs/queries
- **Rule:** Every action with status "done" gets reported on, every Tuesday, until either (a) expected impact observed or (b) 6 weeks passed without movement
- **Severity:** n/a (reporting, not detection)
- **Impact:** n/a
- **Effort:** n/a
- **Suggested action:** report status + observed impact + recommended next step
- **Example wording:** *"Last week's title rewrite on `/page` resulted in CTR moving from 2.1% to 3.4% on the primary query (+47%). Sustaining."*
- **False positive risks:** declaring impact before statistical sample is sufficient
- **Manual QA:** apply a minimum 7-day post-change observation window before claiming impact; longer (21–28 days) for ranking changes

**Manual workflow note:** all detectors should be implemented as spreadsheet formulas + filter rules first. Build one tab per detector. The brief is assembled from those tabs. AI is used only to convert filtered findings into clean prose — never to generate findings.

## 11. Manual delivery SOP

> Goal: produce one finished brief in **90 minutes or less** by week 4.

### Weekly workflow (per site)

**Sunday (5 min):**
- Confirm tomorrow's data pull is on the calendar
- Re-check action log from prior weeks

**Monday AM (15 min):**
- Pull GSC: clicks, impressions, CTR, position by query and by page; 7d / 14d / 28d / 90d / 365d
- Pull GA4: sessions, engaged sessions, key events, landing pages, 14d / 28d
- Drop into the master spreadsheet

**Monday PM (30 min):**
- Run 11 detectors against the data tab
- Flag anything that requires manual SERP check
- Capture screenshots / SERP context for top 3 risks

**Tuesday AM (30 min):**
- Draft brief sections using template
- Use AI assist on prose only (never on detection)
- Sanity-check every recommendation against source data

**Tuesday late morning (10 min):**
- QA checklist (below)
- Apply white-label
- Export PDF, Google Doc, email summary
- Send

### Time estimate per site
- **Site 1 (week 1):** 4–6 hours (template calibration, baseline build, deep familiarization)
- **Site 1 (weeks 2–4):** 2–3 hours, dropping to 90 min by week 4
- **Per additional site after stabilization:** 75–90 min

### Tools

| Purpose | Tool |
|---|---|
| Data pull | Search Console API or manual export (CSV); GA4 API or manual export |
| Spreadsheet | Google Sheets (one workbook per site) |
| Detection rules | Sheets formulas + conditional formatting + filter views |
| AI drafting | Claude / GPT for prose generation only (templates provided) |
| Report assembly | Google Docs template → PDF export |
| Delivery | Email + Google Drive shared folder |
| Action tracking | Single "Action Log" tab per site workbook |

### Step-by-step process

1. **Pull GSC data**
   - Export: Performance → Pages → last 7d, 14d, 28d, 90d (4 separate exports per period: clicks, impressions, CTR, position)
   - Export: Performance → Queries → same periods
   - Save to `/sites/[site]/data/[week-date]/gsc/`
2. **Pull GA4 data**
   - Explore → Free form: dimensions = Landing page, metrics = Sessions, Engaged sessions, Engagement rate, Key events; date = last 14d and 28d
   - Export CSV → `/sites/[site]/data/[week-date]/ga4/`
3. **Load into master sheet**
   - Paste into raw data tabs
   - Detection tabs auto-populate via formulas
4. **Run detectors**
   - Tab 1: Traffic drop — filter view: `recent_clicks_ratio < 0.70 AND recent_vs_28d < 0.75 AND abs_delta >= 30`
   - Tab 2: CTR opportunity — filter: `impressions >= 500 AND ctr_observed < 0.5 * ctr_expected AND position BETWEEN 3 AND 15 AND branded = FALSE`
   - Tab 3: Near-win keywords — filter: `position BETWEEN 4 AND 20 AND impressions >= 100`
   - Tab 4: Content decay — filter: `clicks_28d / avg_clicks_90d < 0.7 AND historical_clicks >= 500 AND seasonal = FALSE`
   - Tab 5: Cannibalization — query → count of pages with >= 50 impressions; flag where count > 1
   - Tab 6: Internal link gaps — separate one-time site map; refreshed monthly
   - Tab 7: GA4 mismatch — pages where `engagement_rate > site_avg AND conversion_rate < 0.25 * site_avg`
   - Tab 8: Refresh candidates — content decay + age > 12 months + time-sensitive flag (manual)
   - Tab 9: Expansion candidates — same page has 3+ related queries in pos 10–25
   - Tab 10: New content — queries in pos 20–50 with rising impressions, no targeting page
   - Tab 11: Action log — manual, updated post-delivery
5. **Manual SERP checks (15 min)**
   - For the top 3 risks and top 5 opportunities, open the SERP in incognito for the relevant query
   - Verify SERP features, competitor changes, intent match
6. **Draft brief sections**
   - Use the Google Docs template (one section per detector)
   - AI prompt: *"Convert this row of detected data into a 2-sentence client-readable bullet. Source data: [paste row]. Do not invent metrics. Do not recommend anything not implied by the data."*
   - Human reviews every line
7. **Build action list**
   - Pick top 5–10 items across detectors
   - Score each P/I/E
   - Sort by Priority desc, Impact desc, Effort asc
8. **QA pass**
   - Run the QA checklist (below)
9. **Apply white-label, export, deliver**
   - Replace branding, export PDF, write email summary, send

### How to export from GSC
- Performance report → set date range → "Pages" tab → Export → CSV
- For per-query-per-page detail: API pull or use the "URL inspection" + "Queries" tab; the Search Console API gives this cleanly with paging
- For higher data volume: link GSC to BigQuery (free, daily export)

### How to export from GA4
- Explore → Free form report → set dimensions and metrics → set date range → export CSV
- For higher data volume: GA4 → BigQuery linking (free tier sufficient for most sites)
- Conversion verification: GA4 DebugView during a test event firing

### How to compare periods
- WoW: last 7d vs. prior 7d (days 8–14)
- vs. baseline: last 7d vs. 28d trailing daily average × 7
- vs. 90d: last 28d vs. trailing 90d daily average × 28
- YoY: last 28d vs. same calendar window in prior year

### How to identify opportunities
- Filter by detector rules
- Manually review top results — never copy the entire detector output into the brief
- Top 5 by impact × (6 – effort) → enter brief

### How to write recommendations
- One sentence: what + where (URL/query)
- One sentence: expected impact (range, not a single number)
- One sentence: recommended action (specific enough to assign)
- Never recommend more than 10 items per brief

### How to QA the report

**QA checklist (before send):**
- [ ] Every metric in the brief traces back to a specific row in the data tabs
- [ ] Every URL in the brief actually exists (200, indexed)
- [ ] No recommendations rely on metrics outside the configured access (no implied competitor data, etc.)
- [ ] Seasonality flags applied where relevant
- [ ] Branded queries excluded from CTR rewrites
- [ ] No recommendations on pages the client flagged as off-limits
- [ ] Action log updated with current week's items
- [ ] Previous week's actions all addressed in follow-up section
- [ ] Tone matches agreed agency voice
- [ ] PDF white-label correct (logo present, footer correct, no internal references)
- [ ] Email summary forwardable as-is
- [ ] Math sanity: any %s recomputed from raw numbers

### Avoiding wrong recommendations
- Never recommend a title rewrite on a branded query.
- Never recommend redirecting an indexed page without checking traffic share.
- Never flag a drop without checking if site-wide clicks also fell (Google update).
- Never recommend execution on a page the agency said is off-limits.
- Never claim "decay" on a page that is seasonal or intentionally dated.
- Always include "expected timeframe to observe impact" — SEO is slow.
- If the data is ambiguous, recommend *investigation*, not *execution*.

### Tracking previous actions
- Action log tab is the single source of truth.
- Each action gets: ID, created date, action description, owner, status, expected impact, post-execution observation date, observed result.
- Follow-up section pulls from the action log automatically (`status = done AND observed = pending`).

### Reusable insight templates

Maintain a library of pre-written 2-sentence templates for the most common insight types. Examples:

```
[CTR rewrite]
"`<URL>` ranks position <P> for `<query>` (<imp>/wk imp, CTR <obs>% — expected ~<exp>%). Title rewrite recommendation included. Expected lift: +<low>–<high> clicks/wk."

[Decay]
"`<URL>` clicks down <pct>% vs 90d baseline. Suspected cause: <cause>. Recommended action: <refresh|expand|consolidate|retire>."

[Near-win]
"`<URL>` ranks position <P> for `<query>` (<imp>/wk imp). <Specific action>. Expected: position <P> → <target> within <weeks>w."

[Cannibalization]
"Query `<query>` ranks both `<url-1>` (pos <P1>) and `<url-2>` (pos <P2>). Recommendation: <consolidate|differentiate|301>."

[Internal link]
"`<source>` mentions <topic> but does not link to `<target>`. Add anchor `<anchor>`."

[GA4 mismatch]
"`<URL>` has <sessions> sessions in <period> but only <conv> conversions vs. site average of <expected>. Likely cause: <cause>."
```

Maintain templates per site if voice/style differs.

### Scaling from 1 to 10 sites

| Sites | Strategy |
|---|---|
| 1 | Fully manual, optimize template, build CTR curves |
| 2–3 | Standardize spreadsheet; AI-draft prose; you review |
| 4–5 | Hire a part-time analyst for data pulls; senior reviewer signs off every brief |
| 6–10 | Build a thin internal tool (V1 in roadmap); analyst delivers, you spot-check 1 in 3 |
| 10+ | V1 must exist; you operate as QA + sales |

**Hard rule:** never take on site 4 before site 3 takes <2 hours to deliver. Treadmill kills the business faster than slow sales.

### Objection handling

**"We already use Looker Studio."**
Looker Studio shows data. The brief tells you what to do with it. They're different layers — and the brief actually rolls up into a monthly recap you could feed back into your Looker dashboards. They don't compete.

**"We already use Semrush / Ahrefs."**
Those are research and competitive intel tools. They tell you about the SEO universe. The brief is specifically about this one client's site this week and what to ship next. Most of our agency clients run both.

**"We already send monthly reports."**
You'll send fewer of them and they'll be better. The weekly briefs roll up into a monthly recap automatically, so your account managers spend zero hours assembling a separate monthly. The weekly is what your clients actually want — they just don't know how to ask for it.

**"We do this in-house."**
You do parts of it. The question is: does your senior analyst look at every account every week, or does the junior just paste screenshots? We're the analyst layer your seniors don't have time for, scaled across all your accounts.

**"AI insights are unreliable."**
Agreed — fully agentic SEO reports are unreliable. Ours aren't AI insights. Detection runs on deterministic rules against GSC and GA4 data. AI drafts prose. A human reviews every recommendation. You can verify any line against the source data in the spreadsheet we keep per site.

**"Our clients won't care."**
Some won't. The ones paying $2k–$10k retainers care a lot — they're already complaining about lack of proactive direction. Pick one of those for the pilot.

**"Price is too high."**
$1,600 for 4 weeks is replacing 12–16 hours of senior analyst time at your billable rate. If your senior is at $150/hr, you're spending $2,400 already to produce a worse output. If the price is genuinely the blocker, run it on one site for 4 weeks and decide based on results — refund clause makes the downside zero.

**"We don't want to give access."**
We only request read-only GSC and Viewer-level GA4. Both are revocable in one click. No CMS, no servers, no admin. Most agencies grant this access daily to their own contractors.

**"We only need monthly reports, not weekly."**
The weekly cadence is the product. It's what catches problems while they're fixable, vs. discovering them a month later. We can roll up into a monthly recap for your client, but the weekly detection is what makes the briefs useful.
